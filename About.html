About Endangered Species


Why Protect Endangered Species?

Protecting an endangered species can be costly and time consuming, so why try?

  • For starters, there is the intrinsic value of the creature. They are cool to look at, are used as teaching tools and can be an important cultural symbol depending on the species.

  • Many of the species in danger that are going extinct are in this position because of direct or indirect human activity. For example, we overhunted the Red Wolf to near extinction or when we overpolluted the waters and expanded coastal development we threatened most sea turtles.

  • Some species offer valuable ecosystem services. For example mussels clean pollutants out of the water and bats help keep insect populations down.

  • Many different species have been used for multiple medical breakthroughs.

There are other reasons to protect endangered species in addition to the reasons listed above; however, if these different creatures die out, then all of these possible advantages are gone with them. Extinction is forever, once they are gone, they are gone for good.
