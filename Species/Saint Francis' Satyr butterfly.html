Saint Francis' Satyr Butterfly

(Photos from U.S. Fish and Wildlife service)

Description

Saint Francis' Satyr Butterfly has a wingspan of around 34 - 44mm. It is a brown butterfly with eye-spots on the lower portion of the wings (Service).

Habitat and Location

It is only located in North Carolina in Cumberland and Hoke counties. It likes to live in the Sandhill area in wet meadows (Service).

Reasons For Endangerment

This extremely rare butterfly has lost much of its habitat due to eradicating beavers and suppressing fires both of which are required in order to make a perfect Saint Francis' Satyr butterfly habitat. Also due to how extremely rare it is there is a threat of poaching from collectors. It has gotten to the point where exact colony sites are kept secret and revealed only to other researchers. Otherwise there is a good chance it would be poached to extinction (Service).
