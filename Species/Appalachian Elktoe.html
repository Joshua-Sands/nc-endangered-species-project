Appalachian elktoe

(Photo on left from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife service, right from NatureServe)

Description

The Appalachian elktoe mussel has a thin shell that measures about 10 centimeters in length. The adult's outer shell is normally a dark-brown to greenish-brown color while a juvenile's tends to be yellowish-brown (U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 2016).

Habitat and Location

The Appalachian elktoe can be found in Graham, Haywood, Henderson, Jackson, Macon, Mitchell, Swain, Transylvania, Yancey, and counties in North Carolina. They can also be found in Tennessee (U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 2016).

The mussel is often found in shallow, silt free, fast moving streams with coarse sand and gravel. The elktoe mussel requires a substrate that is stable (U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 2016).

Reasons For Endangerment

The Appalachain elktoe decline began in September of 2004 after hurricanes Frances and Ivan hit North Carolina. After the hurricanes large populations started washing up dead.
