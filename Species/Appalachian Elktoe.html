Appalachian elktoe

(Photo on left from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife service, right from NatureServe)

Description

The Appalachian elktoe mussel has a thin shell that measures about 10 centimeters in length. The adult's outer shell is normally a dark-brown to greenish-brown color while a juvenile's tends to be yellowish-brown (Service).

Habitat and Location

The Appalachian elktoe can be found in Graham, Haywood, Henderson, Jackson, Macon, Mitchell, Swain, Transylvania, Yancey, and counties in North Carolina. It is also possible to find it in state of Tennessee (Service).

The mussel is often found in shallow, fast moving streams with silt-free, coarse sand and gravel. It is important to the elktoe mussel that the substrate is stable (Service).

Reasons For Endangerment

The start of the Appalachain elktoe decline started in September of 2004 after the hurricanes Frances and Ivan hit the North Carolina area. After the hurricanes the population started washing up dead. By 2006 the population had decreased by around 80%. It is suggested that changes in habbitat, runoff, metal toxins, an increase in dieases, and changes in competition from other organisms are possibly responsible for the decline (Miller).
