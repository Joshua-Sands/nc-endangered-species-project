Tar River spinymussel

(Photos on left from U.S. Fish and Wildlife, right from NatureServe)

Description

The Tar river spinymussel is one of three species of freshwater mussels that grow spines on their shells. The maximum shell length for this mussel is 60mm. Adults have a very dark brown outer shell. Younger specimens have an orange-brown outer shell (NatureServe, 2016).

Habitat and Location

The Tar river spinymussel is only found in North Carolina in Edgecombe, Franklin, Halifax, Johnston, Nash, Pitt, and Warren counties (U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 2016).

This mussel will be found in highly oxygenated, fast-flowing water in woodland areas. They prefers a substrate of coarse sand or pea gravel but will settle for finer substrates if they have to (NatureServe, 2016).

Reasons For Endangerment

A massive mussel kill off occurred in 1990 due to organophosphate or carbonate pesticides. Today the Tar river spinymussel faces threats of sediment loading, water pollution, and other seemingly random events such as the river becoming unstable (NatureServe, 2016).
