Carolina Northern Flying Squirrel

(Photo on left from U.S. Fish and Wildlife service, right from U.S.G.S)

Description

This mammal has brown fur on its back that turns to white fur as it reaches the belly. It looks a lot like the southern flying squirrel except that the Carolina Northern Flying squirrel is larger by about a third of its body mass. Like most flying squirrels they have folds of skin that allow them to glide. The folds extends from their wrists to their ankles (Services).

Habitat and Location

In North Carolina the Carolina Northern Flying Squirrel can be found in Avery, Buncombe, Caldwell, Graham, Haywood, Henderson, Jackson, McDowell, Mitchell, Swain, Transylvania, Watauga, and Yancey counties. It can also be found in Tennessee and Virginia.

The Carolina Northern Flying Squirrel is a subspecies of Northern Flying Squirrel. So like most Northern Flying Squirrels they like to live in moist, cool forests at high elevations. They are typically found on hardwood trees (Services).

Reasons For Endangerment

The Carolina Northern Flying Squirrel has had its natural habitat segmented into eight remaining areas. This has led to a decline in their population and reconnecting these areas to each other is one of the most important things in order to help this species bounce back (Loeb).
